01 · Foundation

The Brand

Translation Layer is not a consulting firm. It is a forge — a place where raw capability is worked into precise, deployable output at the intersection of artificial intelligence, technology, and human organization.

We operate in the space where executive intent meets engineering reality and neither fully understands the other. We speak both languages. We build the bridge, then we hand it over. Our work is not advice. It is execution, documented and transferable.

Ethos

Provocative Exacting Relentless

Provocative — We challenge the premise before accepting the brief. We design experiences and arguments that make people ask "how did they do that" and "why doesn't ours feel like this."

Exacting — Precision is not perfectionism. We mean the right thing at the right fidelity for the right moment. Every pixel, every parameter, every word is a decision.

Relentless — We ship things that work at scale. We do not stop at good enough. We stop at done-right-and-proven.

Nautical Foundations

The brand's underlying vocabulary is navigational. This is not decoration — it is a genuine operating grammar rooted in the founder's deep background in sailing and scuba diving. Navigation is the discipline of knowing exactly where you are and where you are going under conditions of incomplete information, changing environment, and real consequence for error.

This maps precisely to the work Translation Layer does.

02 · The Mark

The Fix

In navigation, a fix is not an estimate. It is a confirmed position obtained by taking simultaneous, independent bearings from multiple known sources — range rings from a chart converging to a single, unambiguous point. Four bearings agree. You know where you are.

Translation Layer is the fix. Four domains — Business, People, Technology, and AI — each provide their bearing. The mark records where they converge. The intersection is not a compromise between the four. It is the only place from which all four can be seen clearly at once. That is the Translation Layer.

Geometric System

The mark is built from four overlapping circles arranged in a 2×2 grid. Each circle is rendered as a 105° arc — the inward-facing portion only — oriented toward the mark's center. The arcs of all four circles converge at the center without touching, implying a fix point rather than marking it explicitly.

The Center Treatment

Default: No Center

The mark has no center element in its resting state. Translation Layer is the space between, not a point to be named. The four arcs converge toward (50,50) without touching — the fix is implied, not declared.

Converge: Compass

Under attention — hover states, focus, active interactions, selected states — a 4-pointed navigator's compass star appears at center. The fix is confirmed. This is the Converge animation state.

The Two-Tone System

The mark encodes a fundamental tension in Translation Layer's work: the warm human domains of Business and People against the cool systematic domains of Technology and AI. This is made explicit in the Two-Tone color variant.

Brass Domains
Business · People

The warm domains. Human organizations, human relationships. The client context we enter. Rendered in brass — the material of navigation instruments, precision tools, architectural craft.

Steel Domains
Technology · AI

The cool domains. Systems, code, models, inference. The machine context we work in. Rendered in steel — the material of precision instruments' inner workings, cold and exact.

The center stays brass. Translation Layer is a human endeavor, regardless of what it builds. The fix is ours to confirm — not the machine's.

05 · Animation

Earned Motion

"Every animation earns its existence. Reveals build anticipation, transitions maintain spatial awareness. Nothing loops without reason."

The mark has four animation states. Each exists for a specific contextual purpose. None is decorative. The level of motion craft is proof of Translation Layer's capability — not decoration of it.

Draw In
Entrance · Page load · Section reveal
Arcs appear sequentially in domain order: Business → People → Technology → AI. Stagger: ~185ms. The sequence encodes the brand argument — human foundations before machine extension. Business arc at full opacity first (confident, solid); AI arc last (emergent, becoming). Total duration: ~800ms.
Breathe
Idle loop · Background state
Four arcs pulse subtly out of phase, creating a slow rotating opacity wave. Each arc's opacity oscillates ±2.5% offset by π/2 radians from its neighbors. Period: 3.2s. The mark is alive and attentive without demanding attention.
Converge
Hover · Focus · Active / selected state
The compass center fades in at (50,50), confirming the fix under attention. Duration: 300ms ease-out. The mark "finds its fix" when examined. Release → center fades (200ms). This is the only moment the center element appears in interactive UI contexts.
Scatter
Exit · Dismiss · Route transition
Arcs retract in reverse order: AI → Technology → People → Business. The human domains hold longest. Stagger: ~150ms between each. Duration per arc: ~190ms. Total: ~650ms. Business arc is the last element present before the mark disappears.

07 · Usage

Guidelines

Clear Space

Minimum clear space around the mark on all sides: 20% of the mark's rendered height. At 100px, this is 20px on each side. At 32px, 6.4px. Nothing may intrude into this zone: other marks, text, photography, edges.

Minimum Sizes

VariantMinimum Display SizeReasoning
Full Detail (Distinct Strokes)48pxBelow this, stroke pattern distinctions disappear
Favicon Edition (Unified)14pxArc silhouette holds to approximately this size

Permitted Usage

  • Use on The Vault background (#1C1914) with Brass or Two-Tone variant
  • Use on The Studio background (#F0EBE1) with light Brass or Two-Tone variant
  • Use reversed (white) on any solid dark surface meeting 4.5:1 contrast
  • Use monochrome black on white or very light surfaces for print
  • Use Compass variant in interactive states only (hover, focus, selected)
  • Scale proportionally only — maintain viewBox aspect ratio (1:1)
  • Favicon Edition at ≤32px exclusively

Never Do This

  • Fill the arc shapes — they are strokes only, always
  • Add drop shadows, glows, emboss, or any effects layer
  • Stretch, skew, or modify proportions in any dimension
  • Reorder or reassign the domain stroke personalities
  • Use on photographic or patterned backgrounds without a solid panel
  • Combine with other brand marks in proximity without brand review
  • Use the Compass center in resting/non-interactive states
  • Recreate or approximate the mark from scratch — use the provided SVG files
  • Use any color other than those in the approved palette on the arcs
  • Use display sizes below the specified minimums
08 · Voice

How We Speak

Translation Layer's voice is the voice of a builder who has been in the room, seen the failure modes, and knows exactly what to do about them. Not cautious. Not hedging. Not performing confidence — actually confident.

Core Attributes

Authoritative
We have done this. We know the failure modes. We lead with the finding, then the reasoning, not the other way around. We do not ask for permission to be right.
Direct
Every sentence has a subject and a verb. Passive voice is a red flag — who does what? We say the hard thing clearly before the soft thing at all.
Specific
We do not say "significant improvement." We say "40% reduction in review cycles." We do not say "leading AI platform." We say which model, what version, what benchmark.
Challenged
We question the premise before accepting the brief. If the question is wrong, the answer does not matter. We say so clearly, then offer the better question.

Language Anti-Patterns

These words and phrases do not appear in Translation Layer's communications. If you find yourself reaching for them, stop and say what you actually mean.

The Test

Before any external communication leaves: Would the CTO of a serious company find this impressive, or would they find it embarrassing?

Serious CTOs read source code. They inspect network tabs. They have seen every consultant's pitch deck. They know immediately when something was written to sound impressive rather than to say something true. Write for that reader.

09 · Digital

Interface Principles

The website does not explain what Translation Layer does. It proves it by existing. A CTO who views source should be impressed. A designer who inspects the animations should ask how.

Motion Principles

  • Every animation earns its existence — justify it or remove it
  • Reveals build anticipation — content enters with purpose, not nervousness
  • Transitions maintain spatial awareness — users always know where they are
  • Nothing loops without reason — Breathe loops because the mark is alive, not for decoration
  • WebGL and particle systems create atmosphere, not distraction — subordinate to content
  • All animations respect prefers-reduced-motion — reduce to instant state changes
  • The theme transition (Vault ↔ Studio) uses View Transitions API for a clean cross-fade

Anti-References

The following are active anti-targets. If the work begins to resemble any of these, the direction is wrong.

  • Generic SaaS landing pages — stock photos, gradient blobs, "Get Started Free"
  • Big Four consulting gravitas — safe, forgettable, committee-designed
  • Startup hype — buzzwords, empty promises, template aesthetics
  • Landing page builders — anything that looks like it emerged from a system designed to look like everything else
  • AI-generated imagery as principal visual content — we are the experts, we do not use AI art as our proof of AI expertise
