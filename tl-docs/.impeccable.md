## Brand System Scope

This file governs the **Translation Layer landing page** at [translationlayer.ai](https://translationlayer.ai). It defines the website's aesthetic direction, typography (Syne + JetBrains Mono), color system (brass/steel), motion design, and mark animation. It is the design spec for a single artifact: the landing page.

For the complete brand documentation system — covering all communications, client deliverables, and data visualization — see the companion files listed in the [Brand Documentation System](#brand-documentation-system) section at the end of this file.

## Design Context

### Users

**Primary**: C-suite executives and senior decision-makers evaluating consulting partners for AI transformation. They arrive skeptical, time-constrained, and pattern-matching against every other consulting site they've seen. They need to know within seconds that translationlayer operates at a level above the noise.

**Secondary**: CTOs and technical leadership — people who read source code, inspect network tabs, and judge you by your engineering choices. The site must signal three things simultaneously: technical depth (these people build, not just advise), taste (they make the right decisions), and results (they ship things that work at scale).

### Brand Personality

**Provocative. Exacting. Relentless.**

Not a consulting firm — a forge. The voice challenges assumptions and demonstrates bleeding-edge mastery at the intersection of AI, technology, and business. This is not "thought leadership" dressed in a suit. It's a builder's identity expressed through craft so precise it becomes provocation. The site doesn't explain what translationlayer does — it proves it by existing.

### Aesthetic Direction

- **Scope**: Single-page landing. The constraint is the point — say everything that matters in one focused experience.
- **Dual-theme**: Both dark and light modes are fully designed, equal-weight experiences. Users choose their preference; the system also respects `prefers-color-scheme`.
- **Dark mode ("The Vault")**: Deep warm black, luminous brass accent, depth via lighter surfaces. Private members' club for people building the future.
- **Light mode ("The Studio")**: Warm paper ground, deeper brass for contrast, depth via subtle shadows. Architectural studio — clean, structural, typography-forward.
- **Anti-references**: Generic SaaS (stock photos, gradient blobs, "Get Started Free"), corporate bland (safe, forgettable, committee-designed), startup hype (buzzwords, empty promises, template vibes), and traditional consulting gravitas (McKinsey-safe, risk-averse, designed by committee). If it looks like it came from a landing page builder or a Big Four rebrand, it's wrong.
- **Typography**: Syne 800 for display (uppercase, tight tracking), JetBrains Mono 400 for technical detail. The pairing signals both design provocation and engineering credibility.
- **Motion**: GSAP-driven, purposeful, and unapologetic. Every animation earns its existence — reveals build anticipation, transitions maintain spatial awareness, nothing loops without reason. WebGL and particles create atmosphere, not distraction. Theme transitions use the View Transitions API for a smooth cross-fade. The level of motion craft IS the brand — it's proof of capability, not decoration.

### Color System

OKLCH-based two-layer token architecture:

- **Primitive tokens** (`--raw-*`): raw OKLCH values, mode-independent, never used directly in components.
- **Semantic tokens** (`--bg`, `--text`, `--accent`, `--surface`, etc.): role-based, redefined per mode in `[data-theme="light"]`.
- **Accent**: Brass (hue ~70) — refined from gold, connoting craft and architectural precision rather than finance.
- **Structural**: Steel (hue ~240) — cool counterpoint for borders, secondary elements.
- **Neutrals**: All tinted toward brand warmth (hue ~80 for lights, ~300 for darks).
- **Alpha avoided** except for overlay/glow uses where see-through is the intent. All text, surfaces, and borders use explicit OKLCH values per mode.

### Brand Identity — The Mark ("The Fix")

A geometric navigational instrument built from four 105-degree arcs (R=29, viewBox 0 0 100 100). Each arc represents a domain of Translation Layer's practice:

- **Business** (top-left, solid stroke, sw 2.2) — the foundation, drawn first
- **People** (top-right, dotted stroke 2/2.5, sw 1.4) — human connection
- **Technology** (bottom-left, dashed stroke 7/3, sw 1.5) — systematic implementation
- **AI** (bottom-right, fine dots 1.5/2.5, sw 1.0) — the frontier, drawn last

The mark encodes a thesis: human foundations (Business, People) drawn before machine extensions (Technology, AI). The draw sequence IS the brand argument.

**Nautical Foundations**: The entire brand vocabulary draws from celestial navigation. A "fix" is a confirmed position from multiple lines of position. Translation Layer helps organizations find their fix — their confirmed position at the intersection of business need, human capability, technical reality, and AI potential. Other vocabulary: LOP (line of position), bearing, chart, dead reckoning, brass instrument.

**Two-Tone System**: Brass arcs for Business + People (warm, human, craft). Steel arcs for Technology + AI (cool, systematic, precision). The mark is the visual thesis of "two metals, two temperatures, one tension." The compass center is always brass — "Translation Layer is a human endeavor."

**Center Treatment**: No center at rest. Compass star appears on hover/focus/active only (the "Converge" state). Center fill is always brass via `var(--accent)`.

### Mark Animation States

Four states with precise timing specs validated against motion design best practices:

- **Draw In** (entrance): Arcs appear sequentially Business → People → Tech → AI. 260–345ms per arc, ~185ms stagger. Easing: `cubic-bezier(0.22, 1, 0.36, 1)` (ease-out-quint). Total: ~800ms. Overdrive: trailing glow edge on each arc.
- **Breathe** (idle loop): Four arcs pulse opacity ±2.5% out of phase (π/2 offset between neighbors). Period: 3.2s. GPU-only (`opacity` property, `will-change: opacity` on arcs). Overdrive: luminance flow via `stroke-dashoffset` animation on overlay paths.
- **Converge** (hover/focus): Compass center fades in. 300ms ease-out in, 200ms ease-in out (67% exit/enter — snappy for micro-interaction). Arcs tighten inward ~2%. Cursor bearing locks to 0°.
- **Scatter** (exit): Arcs retract in reverse (AI → Tech → People → Business). ~190ms per arc, ~150ms stagger. Total: ~650ms (~80% of Draw In). Easing: `cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 1, 1)`.

### Voice

**Attributes**: Authoritative, Direct, Specific, Challenged. Speaks from experience, not theory. Shows work. Challenges assumptions. Names specific technologies, specific trade-offs, specific outcomes.

**Anti-pattern words** (never use): synergy, leverage (as verb), disrupt, thought leadership, best-in-class, world-class, cutting-edge, next-generation, turnkey, scalable (without specifics), innovative (without evidence), transform (without before/after).

### Typography Hierarchy

Complete 8-role type scale:

| Role | Family | Weight | Size | Case | Tracking |
|------|--------|--------|------|------|----------|
| Display / Hero | Syne | 800 | 48–72px | Uppercase | −0.01em |
| Section Heading | Syne | 800 | 28–36px | Title case | −0.01em |
| Sub Heading | Syne | 800 | 14–16px | Uppercase | +0.04em |
| Eyebrow / Label | JetBrains Mono | 400 | 9–11px | Uppercase | +0.14em |
| Body | JetBrains Mono | 400 | 13–14px | Sentence case | 0 |
| Caption / Spec | JetBrains Mono | 400 | 11–12px | Sentence case | +0.04em |
| Code / Value | JetBrains Mono | 500 | 11–12px | As-is | 0 |
| Wordmark | Syne | 800 | Variable | Uppercase | +0.13em |

**Rules**: Never italic, oblique, condensed, expanded. Only weights 400, 500, 800. Only Syne and JetBrains Mono. The wordmark tracking (+0.13em) is distinct from hero display tracking (−0.01em) — these are different typographic roles.

### Color Hex Anchors

Authoritative reference points anchoring the OKLCH token system:

- **Dark (Vault)**: bg `#1C1914`, text `#E2D8C4`, brass `#C4973A`, steel `#7A90AA`
- **Light (Studio)**: bg `#F0EBE1`, text `#1C1610`, brass `#9A6E1A`, steel `#4E708A`

### Accessibility

Target WCAG 2.1 AAA compliance. The premium experience must be inclusive: `prefers-reduced-motion` fully honored, high contrast ratios maintained across both themes, keyboard navigation complete, screen reader semantics correct. Accessibility is not a concession — it is evidence of craft.

### Design Principles

1. **Earned attention** — Every visual element, animation, and interaction must justify its presence. If it doesn't serve comprehension or emotional resonance, remove it.
2. **Craft is the argument** — The code IS the portfolio. WebGL shaders, GSAP choreography, performance optimization, and accessibility are not features — they're proof of what translationlayer delivers. A CTO who views source should be impressed.
3. **Provoke, don't pander** — Challenge the visitor's expectations of what a consulting site can be. No safe choices, no hedge-your-bets design. The site should make people ask "how did they do that?" and "why doesn't our site feel like this?"
4. **No templates, no shortcuts** — Every pixel is intentional. If a pattern feels borrowed from a SaaS landing page or a consulting template, rethink it from first principles.
5. **Inclusive by default** — Premium does not mean exclusive. Reduced motion, screen readers, keyboard navigation, and semantic HTML are non-negotiable foundations, not afterthoughts.
