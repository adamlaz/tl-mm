# Pragmatic Engineer Test — Mad Mobile

**What this measures:** Engineering culture maturity — a modern 12-question yes/no check created by Gergely Orosz (The Pragmatic Engineer) as a successor to Joel Spolsky's classic "Joel Test."

**Time:** ~2 minutes
**Who takes this:** 3–5 engineering leads or senior engineers. Answer for your team/area.
**Anonymity:** Your responses are anonymous. Only aggregate patterns are reported — nothing is attributed by name.
**Scoring:** 10+ out of 12 = mature engineering culture. Below 6 = red flag.

---

## Instructions

For each question, answer **Yes** or **No** based on how your team actually operates today. Don't answer based on how things are supposed to work or what's written in a wiki — answer based on what actually happens.

## Scoring Guide (for Adam's analysis)

| Score | Assessment |
|---|---|
| 11–12 | Mature engineering culture — strong foundation |
| 8–10 | Solid but gaps exist — identify the specific "No" answers |
| 5–7 | Significant culture and process gaps — systemic issues likely |
| 0–4 | Foundational engineering practices are missing — urgent attention needed |

**Segmentation:** Compare scores across teams. A team scoring 10 next to a team scoring 4 tells a story about uneven investment and support.

*Source: Gergely Orosz, "The Pragmatic Engineer Test," The Pragmatic Engineer Newsletter*
